Teriyaki Salmon

Description

Ingredients

  • 1 and 1/4 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1 and 1/2 pounds of salmon fillets
  • 1 medium green onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 teaspoons of toasted sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Mix 1/4 cup of cold water with cornstarch in a small bowl with a lid. Seal the bowl and shake it until the cornstarch is fully dissolved and set it aside.
  2. Combine the remaining cup of water with the soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and sesame oil in a saucepan over medium-high heat until it's brought to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat to a simmer before stirring in the cornstarch mixture. Keep simmering for about 5 minutes so the sauce can thicken, then take it off the heat and set aside 1/2 cup of the glaze you just made.
  4. Set an oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's boiler to low. Place your salmon on a foil-lined baking sheet. Brush all sides of it with some of your remaining glaze.
  5. Broil the salmon for 12 minutes, then brush that 1/2 cup of glaze over the top. Turn the broiler up to high and broil for about 3 minutes or until the salmon flakes easily. Garnish it with the green onion and sesame seeds.
